# ITU-T Working Methods -- extracted from Rec. ITU-T A.1 (09/2019)

## Organizational structure

### ITU-T
The ITU Telecommunication Standardization Sector is a permanent organ of ITU.
It studies technical, operating and tariff questions and issues Recommendations.

### WTSA
The World Telecommunication Standardization Assembly meets every four years,
establishes topics for study by ITU-T study groups.

### TSAG
The Telecommunication Standardization Advisory Group advises on priorities,
coordination, and the work programme between WTSA meetings.

### TSB
The Telecommunication Standardization Bureau (Director + staff) provides
secretariat support: circulates documents, organizes meetings, publishes
Recommendations.

### Study groups
Study groups meet to facilitate approval of Recommendations. They:
- Establish work programmes with target dates
- Hold meetings with TSB Director approval
- May entrust Questions to working parties

### Working parties
A study group may entrust a Question, group of Questions, or maintenance of
existing Recommendations to a working party. Tasks can be further divided
into sub-working parties.

### Rapporteur groups
Rapporteurs are appointed to coordinate detailed study of individual Questions.
They work primarily by correspondence and may hold rapporteur group meetings.
A rapporteur may appoint associate rapporteurs, liaison rapporteurs, or editors.

### Other groups
- **Focus Group (FG):** Forward-looking; encourages non-member participation;
  does not write Recommendations. See ITU-T A.7.
- **Intersector Rapporteur Group (IRG):** Coordinates between ITU sectors
  (ITU-T, ITU-R, ITU-D).
- **Joint Coordination Activity (JCA):** Coordinates across study groups on
  broad topics. Does not perform technical studies or write Recommendations.
- **Regional Group (RG):** Regional coordination. See WTSA Res. 54.

## Key terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Question** | Description of an area of work to be studied, normally leading to new or revised Recommendations |
| **Work item** | An assigned piece of work identifiable with a Question, with specific objectives resulting in a product (e.g. a Recommendation) |
| **Work programme** | A list of work items owned by a study group; maintained in a searchable database |
| **Recommendation** | The normative output of ITU-T study groups; approved via TAP or AAP |
| **Amendment** | Changes or additions to an already published Recommendation |
| **Corrigendum** | Corrections to an already published Recommendation (same approval procedure as amendment) |
| **Erratum** | Correction of publication/editorial errors; published by TSB with study group Chairman concurrence |
| **Annex** | Integral material necessary for overall completeness; same approval procedure as the Recommendation itself |
| **Appendix** | Supplementary material not essential to completeness; agreed by study group (not requiring full approval) |
| **Normative reference** | Whole or parts of another document whose provisions, through reference, constitute provisions of the referring document |
| **Clause** | Single-digit or multiple-digit numbered text passages |

## Meeting procedures

### Scheduling
- Meetings require TSB Director approval
- Timetable published at least one year in advance
- Meetings should not be more frequent than necessary for effective progress

### Pre-meeting
- Collective letter with agenda, draft work plan, Question listing sent
  ideally two months before meeting
- Participants should register at least one month before
- Unregistered participants may experience document delays

### Cancellation
- If insufficient contributions received, meeting may be cancelled by
  Director in agreement with chairman

### During meeting
- Chairman directs debates with TSB assistance
- Chairman may decide no discussion on Questions with insufficient contributions
- Questions with no contributions for two consecutive meetings may be deleted
- IPR declaration is requested at each meeting

### Reports
- TSB prepares meeting report (or chairman if TSB not present)
- Should be approved before end of meeting; otherwise by chairman afterward
- Contains: organization of work, contribution summaries, main results,
  status of Recommendations, future work directives, liaison statements

### Rapporteur group meetings
- Convening letter posted at least two months before meeting (TSB template)
- Rapporteur prepares meeting report as a TD for parent group
- Report includes: date, venue, chairman, attendance list with affiliations,
  agenda, summary of technical inputs, summary of results, liaison statements
- IPR question must be asked and recorded

## Roles

### Study group chairman
- Establishes work structure and selects working party chairmen
- Directs meeting debates
- Responsible for promotion/marketing plan

### Rapporteur responsibilities
a. Coordinate detailed study per working party/study group guidelines
b. Act as contact point for other ITU groups, SDOs, TSB
c. Adopt appropriate work methods (correspondence, meetings)
d. Review and update work programme
e. Keep parent group informed of progress
f. Submit progress report as TD to each parent group meeting
g. Submit draft Recommendations as separate TDs at least six weeks before meeting
h. Give advance notice of rapporteur meetings
i. Establish group of active collaborators
j. Delegate functions to associate/liaison rapporteurs

### Editor
- Assists rapporteur in preparing text of draft Recommendations/publications

### Associate rapporteur
- Assists rapporteur generally or with a particular area of study

### Liaison rapporteur
- Ensures effective liaison with other groups by attending meetings,
  correspondence, or other means
