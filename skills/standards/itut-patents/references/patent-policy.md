# ITU-T/ITU-R/ISO/IEC Common Patent Policy

Reference distillation of the Common Patent Policy and its implementation
guidelines. Sources:

- Guidelines for Implementation of the Common Patent Policy for
  ITU-T/ITU-R/ISO/IEC (Edition 5.0, 16 December 2022)
- "Understanding Patents, Competition and Standardization in an
  Interconnected World" (ITU 2014), Chapter 15

## Policy statement (3 paragraphs)

1. **Disclosure**: Any party participating in the work of ITU, ISO or IEC
   should, from the outset, draw the attention of the Director of ITU-TSB
   (or ITU-BR, or CEOs of ISO/IEC) to any known patent or pending patent
   application, either their own or of other organizations. The organizations
   cannot verify validity of such information.

2. **Three licensing scenarios** when a Recommendation | Deliverable is
   developed and patent information has been disclosed:

   - **2.1 (RF / Free of Charge)**: Patent holder negotiates licences free of
     charge on a non-discriminatory basis on reasonable terms and conditions.
   - **2.2 (RAND)**: Patent holder negotiates licences on a non-discriminatory
     basis on reasonable terms and conditions (may require royalties).
   - **2.3 (Refusal)**: Patent holder is unwilling to comply with 2.1 or 2.2;
     the Recommendation | Deliverable shall not include provisions depending
     on the patent.

3. **Written declaration**: Whatever case applies, the patent holder must
   provide a written statement filed at the relevant bureau using the Patent
   Statement and Licensing Declaration Form. The statement must not include
   additional provisions, conditions, or exclusion clauses beyond what the
   form provides.

## Key definitions

- **Patent**: Claims in patents, utility models and similar statutory rights
  solely to the extent that such claims are **essential** to implementation of
  a Recommendation | Deliverable. "Essential" means required to implement.
- **Free of Charge**: No monetary compensation (no royalties, no one-time
  fees). The patent holder may still require a licence agreement with
  reasonable non-monetary terms (governing law, field of use, warranties).
- **Reciprocity**: The patent holder is only required to license if the
  prospective licensee commits to license its own SEPs for the same
  Recommendation on RF or RAND terms.
- **RAND** (Reasonable And Non-Discriminatory): Also known as FRAND (Fair,
  Reasonable And Non-Discriminatory). The terms are left to the parties;
  the SDO does not define "reasonable."
- **SEP** (Standard-Essential Patent): A patent that would be required to
  implement a specific standard.

## Disclosure rules

- Applies to **any party participating** in ITU work (study groups,
  subordinate groups, other expert groups, national position processes).
- Non-participants may also disclose but are under no obligation.
- "From the outset" = as early as possible during development. First draft
  text may be too vague; disclosure should occur as soon as it becomes clear
  that a draft will include patented elements.
- Information provided **in good faith on a best-effort basis**; no
  requirement for patent searches.
- **Third-party patents**: Participants should also disclose patents of
  other organizations they are aware of. ITU will contact the third party
  and request a Declaration Form. Third parties not participating are not
  obligated to respond.
- **Post-approval discovery**: The policy applies equally to patents
  discovered after a Recommendation is approved. The patent holder is
  requested to provide the same assurances. If unwilling, the Recommendation
  must be revised or withdrawn.

## Declaration forms

### Individual Patent Statement and Licensing Declaration Form

- Submitted per Recommendation (not per study group or meeting).
- Sent to Director of TSB (for ITU-T) or BR (for ITU-R).
- Select **one** of three licensing options (see Policy statement above).
- Option 3 requires additional information: granted patent number or
  application number, affected portions of the document, patent description.
- Multiple forms allowed for the same Recommendation (different patents in
  different options, or different claims of a complex patent in different
  options).
- **Irrevocable** once submitted, except:
  - May be superseded by a more favourable form (option 3 to 2 or 1,
    option 2 to 1, unchecking sub-options).
  - Obvious errors (typographical) can be corrected.
- Must not include additional provisions, conditions, or exclusion clauses.
- Patent holder must supply contact information that remains valid over time.
  Generic name/department and email preferred.

### General Patent Statement and Licensing Declaration Form

- **Voluntary** blanket declaration covering all of an organization's
  contributions to ITU work.
- Not a replacement for the individual Declaration Form.
- Does not imply SEP disclosure (companies can submit even without known
  SEPs).
- Two options (RF or RAND; no refusal option).
- Remains in force unless superseded by a more favourable General Form.
- Individual Declaration Forms for specific Recommendations may contain
  different (including more favourable) terms than the General Form.

## Conduct at meetings

- Chairmen ask at each meeting whether anyone has knowledge of patents
  whose use may be required to implement the Recommendation under
  consideration (for AAP consent or TAP approval).
- The fact the question was asked is recorded in the meeting report, along
  with any affirmative responses.
- Technical bodies may discuss including patented material but **must not
  take position** on essentiality, scope, validity, or specific licensing
  terms of any claimed patents.
- As long as TSB has received no indication of option 2.3 (refusal), the
  study group should proceed with consent or approval.

## ITU Patent Information database

- Publicly available at https://www.itu.int/ITU-T/ipr/
- Contains all declarations submitted via Declaration Forms and General
  Forms.
- **Not certified as accurate or complete** -- only reflects information
  communicated to ITU.
- Best viewed as raising flags alerting users to contact organizations that
  have submitted declarations.
- Cover sheets of all new/revised Recommendations must include a notice
  urging users to consult the database.

## Cover sheet notification text

All new and revised ITU-T Recommendations shall include:

> ITU draws attention to the possibility that the practice or
> implementation of this Recommendation may involve the use of a claimed
> Intellectual Property Right. ITU takes no position concerning the
> evidence, validity or applicability of claimed Intellectual Property
> Rights, whether asserted by ITU members or others outside of the
> Recommendation development process.
>
> As of the date of approval of this Recommendation, ITU [had/had not]
> received notice of intellectual property, protected by patents, which
> may be required to implement this Recommendation. However, implementers
> are cautioned that this may not represent the latest information and
> are therefore strongly urged to consult the ITU Patent Information
> database.

## Transfer of patent rights

- Licensing declarations (option 1 or 2) are interpreted as encumbrances
  binding all successors-in-interest.
- A patent holder transferring ownership of a declared SEP must include
  provisions in transfer documents ensuring the licensing declaration binds
  the transferee.
- The transferee must similarly include provisions in future transfers.
- Compliance discharges the transferor's obligations; no duty to compel
  compliance by the assignee.

## ITU neutrality principles

ITU does not:

- Evaluate patent relevance or essentiality
- Interfere with licensing negotiations
- Settle disputes between patent holders and implementers
- Define what constitutes "reasonable" terms
- Take positions on validity or applicability of claimed IPR

These principles are shared with ISO and IEC and are consistent with the
approach of other major SDOs (ANSI, IEEE, CEN/CENELEC).

## Software copyright and marks

In addition to the Patent Policy, ITU has adopted:

- **Software Copyright Guidelines**: For source code included in
  Recommendations.
- **Guidelines on Marks**: For trademarks, service marks, and certification
  marks incorporated in Recommendations.

These are separate from the Patent Policy but follow similar principles.

## Practical checklist for contributors

When contributing to ITU-T standardization work:

1. **Before the meeting**: Review your contribution for any known patents
   (your own or third-party). If patents are involved, prepare a Declaration
   Form.
2. **At the meeting**: Respond to the Chairman's patent disclosure question.
   Disclose any known patents or pending applications.
3. **After consent/approval**: If you discover patents after a Recommendation
   is approved, submit a Declaration Form to TSB promptly.
4. **General Form**: Consider submitting a General Patent Statement as
   blanket coverage for all your contributions.
5. **Cover sheet**: Ensure the patent notification text is included in any
   Recommendation draft you help prepare.
