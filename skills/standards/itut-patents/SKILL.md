# ITU-T Patent Policy and IPR Procedures

## Overview

ITU-T, together with ITU-R, ISO and IEC, operates under a **Common Patent
Policy** that governs how patented technology is handled in international
standards. The policy is commitment-based: it requires disclosure of known
patents and seeks licensing commitments from patent holders.

**Key principle**: A patent embodied in a Recommendation must be accessible
to everybody without undue constraints. ITU does not evaluate patent
essentiality, interfere with negotiations, or settle disputes.

## When this skill applies

- Writing or reviewing a contribution that may involve patented technology
- Adding patent notification text to a Recommendation draft
- Understanding what happens when a patent is disclosed during development
- Filling in or advising on Patent Statement and Licensing Declaration Forms
- Responding to the Chairman's patent disclosure question at a meeting
- Handling patent discovery after a Recommendation is approved

## Three licensing options

When a patent is disclosed in relation to a Recommendation, the patent
holder selects **one** option on the Declaration Form:

| Option | Label | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Free of Charge (RF) | Licences granted without monetary compensation; non-monetary reasonable terms allowed |
| 2 | RAND | Licences granted on reasonable and non-discriminatory terms; royalties may apply |
| 3 | Refusal | Unwilling to license; the Recommendation must not depend on the patent |

- Option 1 sub-options: may condition on **reciprocity**; may reserve right
  to offer RAND terms to royalty-bearing reciprocal licensees.
- Option 2 sub-option: may condition on **reciprocity**.
- Option 3 requires: patent number, affected portions, patent description.

## Disclosure obligations

- **Who**: Any party participating in ITU work.
- **When**: "From the outset" -- as early as possible during development.
  Also applies to patents discovered **after** approval.
- **What**: Known patents or pending applications, own or third-party.
- **How**: Submit a Declaration Form to the Director of TSB. Best-effort
  basis; no patent search required.
- **At meetings**: Chairmen ask about known patents at each meeting.
  Affirmative responses are recorded in the meeting report.

## Declaration Forms

### Individual form (per Recommendation)

- Select one licensing option.
- Multiple forms allowed for the same Recommendation.
- **Irrevocable** once submitted; can only be superseded by a more
  favourable declaration.
- No additional provisions or conditions allowed beyond the form's options.

### General form (blanket)

- Voluntary; covers all of an organization's contributions.
- Two options only (RF or RAND; no refusal).
- Does not replace the individual form for specific Recommendations.
- Remains in force until superseded by a more favourable General Form.

## Cover sheet notification

Every new or revised Recommendation must include this notice:

> ITU draws attention to the possibility that the practice or
> implementation of this Recommendation may involve the use of a claimed
> Intellectual Property Right. ITU takes no position concerning the
> evidence, validity or applicability of claimed Intellectual Property
> Rights, whether asserted by ITU members or others outside of the
> Recommendation development process.
>
> As of the date of approval of this Recommendation, ITU [had/had not]
> received notice of intellectual property, protected by patents, which
> may be required to implement this Recommendation. However, implementers
> are cautioned that this may not represent the latest information and
> are therefore strongly urged to consult the ITU Patent Information
> database.

## Patent Information database

- URL: https://www.itu.int/ITU-T/ipr/
- Not certified as accurate or complete.
- Contains all submitted Declaration Forms and General Forms.
- Used as a flag-raising mechanism; users should contact declaring
  organizations directly.

## ITU neutrality

ITU does **not**:

- Evaluate patent relevance or essentiality
- Define what constitutes "reasonable" terms
- Interfere with licensing negotiations
- Settle disputes between parties
- Take positions on IPR validity or applicability

## Practical rules for drafting

1. If your contribution proposes text that may require a patent to
   implement, disclose this at the meeting and submit a Declaration Form.
2. Always include the patent notification text on the cover sheet of any
   Recommendation draft.
3. Check the "had/had not received notice" wording against the TSB
   circular for the specific Recommendation.
4. Never add licensing conditions beyond the three options on the form.
5. If you discover a patent after approval, submit the form promptly --
   the Recommendation may need revision if you select option 3.
