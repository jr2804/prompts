# ITU-T Contributions & TDocs -- extracted from Rec. ITU-T A.1 (09/2019) clause 3

## Contribution submission

### Who can submit

Member States and other duly authorized entities registered with a study
group or its relevant groups.

### What contributions contain

Comments, results of experiments, and proposals designed to further the
studies to which they relate.

### Submission method

Via electronic means, in accordance with TSB Director guidance.

### Deadlines

| Timing | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| >= 2 months before meeting | May be translated; posted on web as soon as practicable; printed copies distributed at meeting only to participants who request them |
| 12 calendar days to < 2 months | Cannot be translated; still accepted for meeting |
| < 12 calendar days | Will not appear on agenda; not distributed; held for next meeting (exceptions: contributions of extreme importance may be admitted at shorter notice by Director, with final decision by meeting) |

### Presentation rules

Contributions must follow the presentation and form rules in ITU-T A.2
clause 2. TSB Director may return non-compliant documents to the contributor.

### Web posting

Contributions should be posted on the web within 3 working days of receipt.

### Multiple study groups

Submit to a single study group primarily concerned. If relevant to others,
send a single sheet with title, source, and summary to the other groups.

## IPR and patent declarations

### Patent disclosure

Early disclosure of patent information is desired (Common Patent Policy for
ITU-T/ITU-R/ISO/IEC). Use the "Patent Statement and Licensing Declaration Form"
available at the ITU-T website.

### General patent statement

A voluntary general licensing declaration may be submitted covering all of
an organization's contributions (not per-Recommendation).

### Copyright

By making a contribution, contributors acknowledge that submitted material
has no restrictions that would prevent normal distribution and possible use
in resulting ITU-T Recommendations.

### Software copyright

Contributors submitting copyrighted software must provide a software copyright
statement and licensing declaration form to TSB at the same time.

### External references

If a contribution proposes normative reference to an external document or
incorporates text from it, the source document must be clearly identified.

## TDocs (Temporary Documents)

### What are TDocs

Temporary Documents -- working documents produced during or between meetings.
They include:

- Extracts from reports of other study group meetings
- Reports from chairmen, rapporteurs, or drafting groups
- Proposals from chairmen and vice-chairmen
- Any other meeting-time documents

### Numbering

TDocs are numbered in a separate series from contributions (e.g. `TD 123`).

### Submission deadlines for TDs

| Timing | Rule |
|--------|------|
| >= 7 calendar days before meeting | TDs input before meeting start must be posted no later than 3 working days after receipt, to ensure availability 7 calendar days before meeting |
| < 7 calendar days | Exception for: administrative documents, reports on events < 21 days before meeting, proposals from chairmen/convenors of ad hoc groups, compilations prepared by chairman/secretariat, documents specifically requested by meeting |
| Reports < 21 days before meeting | Should be posted at least 2 calendar days before discussion, unless meeting agrees otherwise |

### During-meeting TDs

TDocs can be produced during the meeting itself.

### Rapporteur TDocs

Rapporteurs should submit each draft new or revised Recommendation as a
separate TD at least six weeks prior to the parent group's meeting.

## Liaison statements

### Purpose

Formal communication between ITU-T groups or with other standards organizations.

### Required information

- Question numbers of originating and destination study groups
- Meeting at which prepared (study group/working party/rapporteur group, date, location)
- Concise title; if reply, make explicit (e.g. "Reply to liaison statement from ...")
- Destination group(s) and/or organizations
- Approval level (study group, working party, or rapporteur group meeting)
- Indicate: for action, for comment, or for information (per destination)
- If action requested: deadline date
- Contact person: name, organization, country, tel, fax, e-mail

### Processing

- Forwarded to destinations as soon after meeting as possible
- Copies to chairmen of involved groups and TSB
- Between meetings: may be prepared by correspondence, approved by study
  group chairman in consultation with management team

### Format example

```
Question(s):  4
Meeting, date: London, 2-6 October 2017
Study Group:  15         Working Party: 1
Source:       ITU-T SG15, Rapporteur group for Q4/15
Title:        LS/o/r on Object identifier registration
              - Reply to liaison statement from Q11/17
              (Geneva, 5-9 February 2017)

LIAISON STATEMENT

For action to:      ITU-T Q11/17
For information to: ITU-R SG11, ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 6
Approval:           Q4/15 rapporteur group meeting (London, 6 October 2017)
Deadline:           22 January 2018
Contact:            John Jones, rapporteur for Q4/15
                    ABC Company, USA
                    Tel: +1 576 980 9987
                    E-mail: jj@abcco.com
```

## Translation

- Contributions received >= 2 months before meeting: may be translated
- If study group agrees to work in original language: no translations
- Contributions received < 2 months but >= 12 calendar days: cannot be translated
- Translation is at TSB discretion, dependent on available resources

## Electronic access

TSB posts all documents (contributions, TDs, liaison statements)
electronically as soon as versions are available. Appropriate search
facilities are provided.
