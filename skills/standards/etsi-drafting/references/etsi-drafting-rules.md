# ETSI Drafting Rules (EDR) -- deliverable structure and conventions
Extracted from: ETSI Directives, ETSI Drafting Rules (EDR), Version June 2025

These rules complement the ETSI Technical Working Procedures (TWP).
They ensure ETSI deliverables are drafted uniformly, irrespective of
technical content. ETSI deliverables shall be drafted on the basis of
ETSI deliverable skeletons available from editHelp!.

---

## 1. ETSI deliverable principles

### Objectives
An ETSI deliverable shall:
- Be as complete as necessary within its scope
- Be consistent, clear and accurate
- Provide a framework for future technological development
- Be comprehensible to qualified persons who have not participated in its preparation
- Respect the ETSI Drafting Rules

### Homogeneity
Uniformity of structure, style and terminology shall be maintained across
all ETSI deliverables. Analogous wording for analogous provisions; identical
wording for identical provisions. Same term for a given concept throughout.

### Language
ETSI deliverables shall be in the English language only. Follow the ETSI
"Use of English" guide.

### Neutrality
ETSI deliverables shall neither promote nor endorse services, products
and/or technologies of one company over another.

## 2. Deliverable structure

### Element arrangement (Table 1)

**Preliminary (informative):**
- Cover page (title, version, date)
- Table of contents (generated)
- Intellectual Property Rights (provided by Secretariat)
- Foreword (includes transposition table for ENs)
- Modal verbs terminology (provided by Secretariat)
- Executive summary (optional)
- Introduction (optional)

**Core (numbered):**
1. Scope
2. References (2.1 Normative, 2.2 Informative)
3. Definitions of terms, abbreviations and symbols (3.1 Terms, 3.2 Symbols, 3.3 Abbreviations)
4+ Technical content (clause 4 onwards)

**Supplementary:**
- Normative annexes (lettered A, B, C...)
- Informative annexes (lettered, continuing sequence)
- Bibliography (optional)
- Change history (required for EN HS, optional otherwise)
- History (provided by Secretariat)

### Cover page
Title composed of up to 3 elements (introductory, main, complementary)
proceeding from general to particular. Multi-part: "Part #: ..." in
complementary element.

### Scope (clause 1)
Required informative element. Defined without ambiguity. Succinct for
bibliographic use. Worded as statements of fact:
- "The present document specifies..."
- "The present document establishes..."
- "The present document gives guidelines for..."
- "The present document is applicable to..."

### References (clause 2)
- 2.1 Normative references: documents whose provisions are required for
  conformance. Must be publicly available in English. Prefer specific
  references (dated) for stability; non-specific (undated) allowed only
  if the referring TC controls the referenced document.
- 2.2 Informative references: documents providing additional information
  but not required for conformance.
- References to EPP deliverables (e.g. 3GPP) must be replaced with
  equivalent ETSI deliverable numbers.

### Definitions, abbreviations, symbols (clause 3)
- 3.1 Terms: alphabetical; definitions shall not contain requirements
- 3.2 Symbols: alphabetical; symbol + explanation
- 3.3 Abbreviations: alphabetical; abbreviation + full form
- Entries in Symbols and Abbreviations shall not be numbered

### Clauses (clause 4+)
- Each clause shall have a title placed after its number
- Numbered subdivisions: 5.1, 5.2, 5.1.1 (up to 6 levels)
- If subdivisions exist, there shall be at least two at each level
- Mixed numbered and unnumbered subdivisions shall NOT be used
- To insert between existing clauses: use alphanumeric (8a)
- To delete: mark as "Void" to preserve numbering
- Automatic numbering may be used with appropriate ETSI styles

### Paragraphs
A paragraph is an unnumbered subdivision. For precise referencing, clauses
shall have either all numbered or all unnumbered subdivisions -- never mixed.

### Annexes
- Start on new page
- Designated: "Annex A (normative):" or "Annex A (informative):" + title
- For entirely informative types (EG, GR, TR, SR): no normative/informative
  indicator needed -- just "Annex A:" + title
- Numbering within annex: A.1, Figure A.1, Table A.1

### Bibliography (optional)
List of documents not referenced in the text but informative for the reader.

### Change history
- Required for EN Harmonised Standards
- Optional for other types
- Records changes per version

## 3. Requirements and provisions

### Verbal forms for expressing provisions

| Verbal form | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **shall** | Mandatory requirement |
| **shall not** | Prohibition |
| **should** | Recommendation |
| **should not** | Recommendation against |
| **may** | Permission |
| **need not** | No requirement (non-mandatory) |
| **can** | Possibility or capability (informative) |
| **cannot** | Impossibility or incapability (informative) |

The "Modal verbs terminology" clause (unnumbered, after Foreword) specifies
these meanings and is provided by the Secretariat.

### Requirements principles (from the Guide)
Each requirement should be:
- **Necessary**: Specify only what is required, not implementation approach
- **Unambiguous**: Impossible to interpret in more than one way
- **Complete**: Contain all information needed to understand it
- **Precise**: Worded clearly and exactly, without unnecessary detail
- **Well-structured**: Elements in appropriate order
- **Consistent**: No contradictions within or across related standards
- **Testable**: Clear means of demonstrating compliance

## 4. Figures, tables, equations, lists, notes

### Figures
- Each figure shall be referenced in the text
- Numbered with Arabic numerals, beginning with 1
- Title below figure, centred: "Figure N: title"
- Notes to figures: placed within the figure frame

### Tables
- Each table shall be referenced in the text
- Numbered with Arabic numerals, beginning with 1
- Title above table, centred: "Table N: title"
- Notes to tables: placed within the table frame

### Mathematical formulae
- Types: display equations (standalone) and inline equations
- Numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals in parentheses
- Layout: equation centred or left-aligned; number right-aligned

### Lists
Three formats (consistent within document):
- **Format A**: items < 1 sentence; lower case, full stop on last only
- **Format B**: longer items; semicolons between items
- **Format C**: each item >= 1 sentence; capitals, full stops on all

### Notes and examples
- Notes integrated in text: "NOTE: " or "NOTE N: " for multiple
- Notes shall not contain requirements (except notes to tables/figures)
- Examples: "EXAMPLE: " or "EXAMPLE N: " for multiple
- Footnotes to text: avoid if possible. If used, numbered consecutively.
  Content shall not contain requirements.

## 5. Editorial and formatting

### ETSI styles
Microsoft Word styles are defined for all ETSI deliverables. The appropriate
ETSI deliverable skeleton (from editHelp!) provides the correct styles.
These address most editorial requirements. Do not add new styles or modify
existing ones.

### Capital letters
- ETSI deliverable referencing itself: "the present document" (lower case)

### Dimensions and tolerances
- Use SI units as far as possible
- Decimal sign: comma or point on the line (be consistent)

### Numbers and numerical values
- Decimal sign consistent within document
- Thousands separated by space (not comma)
- Hard spaces between number and unit

### Pagination
- Page numbering, headers and footers follow the skeleton template

### Sequence numbering and bookmarking
- Use automatic numbering for figures, tables, equations
- Use bookmarks for cross-references within the document

## 6. Technical languages

ETSI supports specialised technical languages where appropriate:
- **SDL** (Specification and Description Language): system behaviour
- **MSC** (Message Sequence Charts): interaction diagrams
- **ASN.1** (Abstract Syntax Notation One): protocol data structures
- **XML**: data representation
- **TTCN-3** (Testing and Test Control Notation): test specifications

---

## 7. Harmonised Standards specifics

- Foreword includes specific text (provided by Secretariat)
- EMC statements required
- Normative references must be specific (dated)
- Annex structure specific to EU regulation requirements
- EN title in official EU languages

---

## 8. Endorsement of documents from other SDOs

ETSI may endorse documents from other standards organisations:
- **Without modifications**: endorsement notice + reference
- **With modifications**: endorsement notice + modifications clause + reference
- Dated: endorsement shall be dated
